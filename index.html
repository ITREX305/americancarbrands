Frequently Asked Questions About American Car Brands

How many American car brands are there?

There are currently around 35â€“40 active American car brands depending on how you define "American" (country of origin vs. country of parent company). Historically, over 1,800 automobile companies have operated in the United States since the early 1900s, though only a fraction built more than a handful of cars. Today, the most prominent active American brands include Ford, Chevrolet, Dodge, Jeep, Ram, GMC, Cadillac, Lincoln, Buick, Tesla, Rivian, and Lucid.

What are the Big Three American car companies?

The "Big Three" refers to Ford Motor Company, General Motors (GM), and Stellantis (formerly Chrysler/FCA). These three companies have dominated American automotive manufacturing since the 1920s. Today, GM operates Chevrolet, Buick, GMC, and Cadillac. Ford operates Ford and Lincoln. Stellantis operates Dodge, Ram, Jeep, and Chrysler in the US market.

What American car brands are no longer made?

Dozens of iconic American brands are now defunct. The most notable discontinued American car brands include Pontiac (2010), Oldsmobile (2004), Saturn (2010), Hummer (2010, revived as EV), Plymouth (2001), Mercury (2011), DeSoto (1961), Packard (1958), Studebaker (1966), and AMC (1988). Each has a fascinating story worth exploring.

What are the best American car brands for reliability?

According to J.D. Power and Consumer Reports data, American brands have significantly closed the reliability gap with Japanese and German competitors. Buick consistently ranks among the most reliable brands in America. Lincoln, Cadillac, and Chevrolet have also shown major reliability improvements in recent years. For reliability-focused buyers, Buick and Lincoln represent strong American luxury choices.

Which American car brands are publicly traded?

Several American car brands have publicly traded parent companies: Ford Motor Company (NYSE: F), General Motors (NYSE: GM), Stellantis (NYSE/Euronext: STLA), Tesla (NASDAQ: TSLA), and Rivian (NASDAQ: RIVN), and Lucid Group (NASDAQ: LCID) are all available on public stock exchanges.

Is Tesla an American car brand?

Yes. Tesla, Inc. is headquartered in Austin, Texas, with major manufacturing facilities in Fremont, California, and a Gigafactory in Austin. While founded with investment from across the globe, Tesla is unambiguously an American company and is the largest American EV manufacturer by volume.
